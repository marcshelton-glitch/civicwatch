# CivicWatch — Voiceover Scripts
*For: Marc N. Shelton, Founder*
*Tone: Measured, urgent, authoritative — like a seasoned journalist breaking a story*

---

## 10-Second Script (≈28 words)

> "Every day, members of Congress cast votes, make deals, and trade stocks — with your money on the line.
> CivicWatch. Now you can see exactly what they're doing."

**Delivery note:** First sentence builds tension. Pause one beat before "CivicWatch." Land the last line with confidence, not urgency.

---

## 30-Second Script (≈85 words)

> "Here's something most people don't know: members of Congress are allowed to trade stocks — individually — while voting on laws that affect those very companies.
>
> Last year alone, they filed over eight thousand trades.
>
> CivicWatch tracks every single one. Voting records. Net worth timelines. Stock trades. Committee memberships. All in one place, updated in real time.
>
> Because democracy only works when citizens know what their representatives are actually doing.
>
> CivicWatch. Know your representatives. Hold them accountable."

**Delivery note:** Open with "Here's something" like you're letting the viewer in on something they should already know but don't. The data stat ("eight thousand") lands harder if you let it breathe — don't rush past it. Slow down for the tagline.

---

## Extended 60-Second Script (≈160 words)
*For a full brand video or YouTube pre-roll*

> "The people we elect to represent us — they make decisions every single day that shape your healthcare, your retirement, your taxes, your future.
>
> But they're also making decisions about something else: their own finances.
>
> In the last Congress, members filed over eight thousand stock trades. Millions of dollars, moving through accounts — sometimes the same week they voted on legislation that affected those very companies.
>
> Most people never see any of it. It's buried in government databases, formatted for lawyers, not citizens.
>
> CivicWatch changes that.
>
> We pull real data from official government sources — financial disclosures, voting records, committee assignments — and make it readable. Searchable. Shareable. In real time.
>
> Your representative just traded Amazon stock. How did they vote on antitrust legislation? Now you can find out in seconds.
>
> This is your country. These are your representatives.
>
> CivicWatch. Know more. Hold them accountable."

**Delivery note:** The opening lines are not an attack — they're an invitation. You're welcoming the viewer into a problem they didn't know they had. The Amazon example should feel like you're showing them something right now, not hypothetically. The closing three lines are the gut punch — let each one land separately.

## Tagline Options (for end cards, social, print)

1. **Know your representatives. Hold them accountable.** *(primary)*
2. **Real data. Real accountability.*
3. **Democracy runs on transparency.**
4. **Your vote. Their record.**
5. **Stop wondering. Start knowing.**

---

## Recording Notes

- **Pace:** Slower than you think. Political content lands harder at ~130 words per minute, not 150+.
- **Environment:** Quiet room, minimal reverb. Lavalier or a decent USB condenser (Blue Yeti, etc.).
- **Tone:** You're not angry. You're the calm person in the room who knows something important. Inform, don't indict.
- **Multiple takes:** Record each script 3 times. The third take is usually the best — you're past self-consciousness.
- **Pickup lines:** Record individual sentences separately so edits are clean.
